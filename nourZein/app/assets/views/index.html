Rooftop Suitability for Green Roof Potential

  • Year of Construction: Buildings before the 1970s have rooftops that can hold more weight, needed for green roofs.

  • Slope Angle: A relatively flat rooftop of slope less than 9.5 degrees is required.

  • Roof Surface Area: Roof areas at least 1,000 ft2 were selected because the larger the area, the greater the environmental benefits outweigh the economic cost. Smaller rooftops can be suitable but are not captured here.
×

Environmental Benefits Explained

  • Heat Mitigation: Cities are hotter than the surrounding suburbs due to concrete and high energy consumption. High temperatures are a public health issue in many cities, and especially in New York.

  • Storm Water Management: Soil absorbs stormwater runoff, unlike concrete- NYC has a Combined Sewer System, and when it is flooded from heavy rain, it spills wastewater into surrounding water bodies.

  • Increased Biodiversity: In order to continue to have clean air, clean water, and a balanced ecosystem, cities need more biodiversity. Green roofs increase biodiversity by providing habitat.
